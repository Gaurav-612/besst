About BESS

A BESS is a group of batteries that stores energy (from the electrical grid or a solar panel system) and automatically adds to your home's energy source when the grid goes down or during on-peak hours.
A Battery Energy Storage system charges and stores power during off-peak and mid-peak hours and then once the system is fully charged, during on-peak hours, the system discharges the stored energy and becomes the home's power source.
During times of peak electricity demand like when air conditioners ramp up on hot days - many utilities charge customers extra since they have to ramp up expensive additional electricity production to meet the demand. These charges are called “time-of-use” or “peak” prices since they are tied solely to when a customer uses the electricity. Energy storage works in similar ways, but since energy storage lets customers decide when to buy and when to use the power, a customer is freed from premium charges, increasing cost savings.

About Results

Since electricity usage will vary by each month of the year - BESST simulates this variation using the average household usage per month in a given geographic location. The dataset can be found through: Average Household Electricity Use by Month!
Similar to the idea of cost-savings, throughout the day - GHG emissions will vary across the grid based on the source of production (solar, nuclear, gas, etc.). If a household were to store electricity during a period of lower GHG emission and then discharge that stored electricity during a higher GHG emission period - then some GHG emission are ‘shaved’ off because electricity from the grid was not used during this time period.

For instance, during off-peak periods - a household may only be using electricity for a small number of appliances (refrigerator, charging devices, etc.).
